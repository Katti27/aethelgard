Era Outline for the Concordance of Ages

## 1.1 — AGE OF CREATION (Timeless Mythic Prehistory)

- 1.1.1 — The Formless Aeon  
Timeless void of **Astra Primordia**; Three Primordial Currents stir.

- 1.1.2 — Birth of the Planes  
The Great Wheel crystallizes: Upper and Lower Planes, Feywild, Shadowfell, Elemental Chaos.

- 1.1.3 — The Shaping of Aethelgard  
Elemental Titans **Akadi, Grumbar, Istishia, Kossuth** forge land, sea, and sky.

- 1.1.4 — The First Gods Take Dominion  
The gods assume stewardship; sun, moon, magic, seasons, and fate are ordered.

- 1.1.5 — The First Sunders  
Mythic cataclysms: Feywild breach, deep caverns torn open, and time disruptions that leave prophetic scars.

---

## 1.2 — THE FIRST PEOPLES (c. –8200 to 5900)

- 1.2.1 — Awakening of the Elves (c. –8170)
Elves awaken in multiple regions of Aethelgard: the western great forest, the western sea-isles, and the far northeastern isles; early kindreds form enduring sites, kin-circles, and oath-customs.

- 1.2.2 — Awakening of the Dwarves (c. –8035)
Dwarves rise within the central mountain roots that will become **Durak-Throndhal, the Gods’ Anvil**; the first hearth-halls and holdfast traditions begin.

- 1.2.3 — Awakening of the Giants (c. –7920)
Giants awaken in the far northern ice and high peaks that will become **Jotunbrud**, while other giant kinds persist in isolated pockets across remote highlands and ranges.

- 1.2.4 — Bronze Guardian of the Western Seas (c. –7810)
A young bronze dragon later known as **Varthash, the Verdant Dream** crosses into Aethelgard and claims **Waveshadow Isle** as a coastal seat, beginning his long role among the western elder peoples.

---

## 1.3 — THE AGE OF FORMATION (c. –5900 to –4200)

- 1.3.1 — Formation of the Emerald Enclave (c. –5890)
The western great forest druid councils formalize under the [[Emerald Enclave]]; the first [[Heartkeeper of the Verdant Heart|Heartkeeper]] is elected; the council names the forest the [[Verdant Heart|Verdant Heart]]; [[Kymarine, the Great Forest City|Kymarine]] is founded as its capital (c. –5865).

- 1.3.2 — Formation of the Tidecallers Circle (c. –5810)
The western sea-isles druid councils formalize under the [[Tidecallers Circle]]; the first [[Tidekeeper of the Tideshae Isles|Tidekeeper]] is elected; they name their home isles the [[Tideshae Isles]]; [[Aqua-El, the Coral Haven|Aqua-El]] is founded as its capital (c. –5795).

- 1.3.3 — Formation of the Kingdom of Thandralas (c. –5740)
Dwarven clans of [[Durak-Throndhal, the Gods’ Anvil|Durak-Throndhal]] unify under the first [[Forge-King of Thandralas|Forge-King]]; the capital [[Khunzar, the Hammerhall|Khunzar]] is created within the volcano [[Durak-Tharum, the Gods’ Forge|Durak-Tharum]] and established as the first royal seat of the [[Kingdom of Thandralas]].

- 1.3.4 — Formation of the Realm of Jotunbrud (c. –5600)
The northern ice-realm frost giants consolidate under the first [[Frost-King of Jotunbrud|Frost-King]], the mightiest of [[Thrym, Lord of the Frost Giants|Thrym’s]] children; within the same year [[Norguhal, the Glacial Fortress|Norguhal]] is raised as the seat of [[Frost-King Hyrm|Frost-King Hyrm’s]] dominion, and the land is named the [[Realm of Jotunbrud]].

- 1.3.5 — Formation of the Mystran Accord (c. –5420)
The elven arcanists of [[Eyllisett, the Isles of Lore]] create the [[Avonnar, the Arcane Citadel|Arcane Citadel Avonnar]] and found the [[Eight Schools of Magic]]. The mages formalize as the [[Mystran Accord]] (c. –5415), establishing the [[Magistratum]] and the [[Council of Avonnar]] for governance and leadership.

## 1.4 — DAWN OF YOUNGER PEOPLES (c. –4250 to –1500)

- 1.4.1 — Fall and Rise of the Dark Seldarine (c. –4250)

- 1.4.2 — Interplanar Invaders (c. –4122 to –3410)

- 1.4.3 — Alien Hunters in the Deep (c. –4122)

- 1.4.4 — Birth of the Dragonforged People (c. –4057)
Dragonborn and kobolds are hatched through divine intervention; other rare lineages appear in scattered, isolated origins.

- 1.4.5 — Gruumsh’s Children (c. –3980)
Orcs and goblinoids are first shaped and set loose across **[[Wild Marches]]** by their deity **[[Gruumsh, the One-Eye|Gruumsh]]**.

- 1.4.6 — Halflings and Gnomes (c. –3830)
**Birth from Gems**: Gnomes believe they were originally formed from gems hidden deep within the earth. Different subraces came from different stones: rock gnomes from diamonds, forest gnomes from emeralds, and deep gnomes from rubies.
**The Mother/Creator Story**: In some traditions, Yondalla, also known as the Provider and the Protector, directly gave birth to the first halflings. Their origins in this early period are still unclear, even to themselves.
Halfling shires form along rivers and gentle hills, while gnome enclaves develop near dwarven delves and regions of wild or experimental magic.

- 1.4.7 — First Awakening of Humanity (c. –3715)
Human tribes appear across temperate plains, river valleys, and coasts—small, scattered, and largely semi-nomadic.

Tieflings, aasimar, and other marked mortal bloodlines arise through pacts, curses, celestial favor, and planar influence, seeding future lineages across the world.

## 1.5 — SCHISMS OF THE FIRST RACES (c. –3600 to –2500 BC)

- 1.5.1 — Shadow Schism Begins (c. –3595)  
A bitter druidic rivalry and religious conflict fractures elven society; exiled elves flee into the deep caverns.

- 1.5.2 — Rise of the Drow Realms (c. –3570 to –2500)  
Exile communities in the depths transform into the first **drow** cities, bound to **Lolth, the Dark Mother**. They carve and claim a growing network of deep passages beneath mountains and lowlands. By the end of the Era a capital city named Menzoberranzan, the City of Spiders, begins to take shape in the Underdark.

- 1.5.3 — Enslavement of the Deep Clans (c. –3410 to –3325)  
Illithids seize isolated dwarf delves in the deep underways; the ancestors of the **duergar** are taken as thralls. Illithid torment and experimentation twist captive dwarves into the grey-skinned duergar, who begin to serve elder brains.

- 1.5.4 — Duergar Rebellion (c. –3325 to –3170)  
**Divine Intervention:** Duergar legends state that their gods, Laduguer and Deep Duerra, were involved in their liberation.
    - **Laduguer** is credited with leading the revolt and purging the influence of other dwarven gods from their souls.
    - **Deep Duerra** is said to have stolen the power of psionics (mind magic) from the mind flayers and gifted it to her people.
**Psionic Abilities and Innate Magic:** Over generations of imprisonment, the Duergar absorbed strange magical power from the Underdark, which, combined with the stolen psionics, gave them unique abilities. They gained resistance to magic, illusions, and charm effects, as well as the ability to enlarge their size and become invisible.
**The Rebellion:** Armed with their new psionic resistance and abilities, the Duergar rose up in a bloody, successful rebellion and massacred their illithid masters.

- 1.5.5 — Consolidation (c. –3170 to –2500)  
The Duergar carve and claim an Empire of their own—growing networks of deep passages and fortified keeps. The illithids retreat to their colonies to lick their wounds and plot their revenge

## 1.8 — AGE OF NEW BEGINNINGS (c. –1150 to –650 BC)

- 1.8.1 — The World After the Migrations (c. –1150)
Human peoples resettle into new valleys, plains, forests, and coasts; long-distance contact becomes intermittent but persistent.

- 1.8.2 – Broadened Mystran Accord (c. –1070 BC)
The Principals of the Eight Schools of Magic cautiously opens to select non-elven spellcasters; dwarven Runesmiths largely maintain their own separate traditions.

- 1.8.2 — The Western Riverlands (c. –1089 to –678)
Settlements begin forming along rivers and expand into trading nodes; boatcraft and overland routes begin linking distant communities.

- 1.8.3 — Eastern Highland Co-operation (c. –1078 to –643)
Populations consolidate into forest ridge-communities on the southeastern peninsula; Friendly relations are established with the local metallic dragonborn inhabitants. Dragonborn introduce humans to the wisdom of Bahamut and the practices of customary law. Both species strive to empower the other in cooperation.

- 1.8.4 — Southern Basins and Coastlands of Ankaram (c. –1054 to –472) 
Populations cohere around arable basins and inland waterways, small towns and markets appear in centralized locations.

- 1.8.5 — The Roaming Clans of the Gilded Plains (c. –1012 to –612)
Formation of the population into roaming horse-clans. Steppe coalitions form, repeatedly unify under strong khans, then fracture; mounted warfare and tribute routes stabilize long-distance exchange.

- 1.8.6 — Forest Lordships of Chornyy Lis (c. –997 to –579)
Populations form around hunting preserves, rivers, and clearings; Forest-bound petty realms form, feud law and oath-bands become customary rule. Vampire's emerge in the lands of Chornyy Lis.

## 1.9 — THE CONTEMPORARY REALMS (c. –650 to –287 BC)

- 1.9.1 — River Casas of Gatoria (c. –678 to –287)
Major merchant families consolidate river fleets, toll rights, and warehouse districts; hereditary houses begin to dominate port politics.

- 1.9.2 — Principalities of the Eastern Highlands (c. –643 to –287)
Authority centers on hillfort seats, river-cuts, and interior roads; law and local courts develop. Local rulers adopt written law-codes and council practice; ports and interior timber-routes increase regional leverage. The earliest orders of oath-knights (future Paladin orders) emerge. 

- 1.9.3 — Khans of Batan (c. –612 to –287)
 Formalization of councils, warbands, and succession traditions under powerful khan-lines. The nomadic tribes begin to establish permanent settlements. The capital city of Batan is founded as towards the end of the Era.

- 1.9.4 — Ducal Houses of Chornyy Lis (c. –579 to –287)
Great houses formalize vassal arrangements and forest jurisdictions; hidden vampiric lineages persist inside noble strata.

- 1.9.6 — Ankaram before the Storm (c. –472 to –287)
Regional rulers and priestly authorities strengthen; ports and inland markets expand influence outward. Selective pacts with elemental powers enter elite practice; genie-aligned patrons gain footholds through courts, shrines, and arcane bargains.

---

## 1.9 — THE AGE OF ELEMENTAL TYRANNY (THE GENIE ERA) (–287 to 0 AC)

- 1.9.1 — Escalation of Elemental Bargains (c. –287 to –274)
Genie influence intensifies across Ankaram’s ruling circles; rival patrons maneuver through mortal proxies.

- 1.9.2 — Summoning Wars in the Basin Courts (c. –273 to –266)
Competing factions weaponize bindings and conjurations; the region destabilizes under escalating elemental reprisals.

- 1.9.3 — Calim and Memnon Ascendant (–266)
The genie-lords seize Ankaram’s heartlands and impose elemental rule.

- 1.9.4 — The Enslavement Regime (c. –266 to 0 AC)
For just over a century forced labor systems, tribute quotas, and overseer hierarchies harden into enduring control. From c. –155 refugees and dissidents slip out by coast and river-mouths, carrying intelligence into northern and eastern courts.  

- 1.9.5 — Elemental Citadels Raised (c. –263 to 0 AC)
Great citadels and seat-fortresses are established across Ankaram to anchor dominion and logistics.

- 1.9.6 — The Skybound Compact (c. –148)
Compact terms are agreed between Ankaram resistance leadership and Avonnar’s Mystran Accord.

- 1.9.7 — Oath-Keepers of the Platinum Dragon (c. –147 to 0 AC)
A Bahamut-aligned oath tradition takes root among Ankaram’s resisting circles, framing protection and just defiance as sacred duty.

- 1.9.8 — The Calimemnon Project (c. –41 to –11)
Binding theory, facet-geometry, and containment principles are developed into a workable plan. The Calimemnon Crystal is forged (–11)—the sixty-faced binding diamond is completed and proven viable as a prison for Calim, Memnon, and their host.

## 1.11 — YEAR 0: UPRISING & THE GENIE CALAMITY (–10 to 0 AC)

- 1.11.1 — Infiltration and Final Conditions (c. –10 to –1)
Cells, routes, and timing are prepared; the plan becomes executable only when all required conditions align.

- 1.11.2 — The Great Uprising (0 AC)
Ankaram’s resistance rises in coordinated revolt as Compact agents strike at key sites.

- 1.11.3 — Calim and Memnon Bound (0 AC)
Calim, Memnon, and the gathered elemental lords are drawn into the Calimemnon Crystal and sealed.

- 1.11.4 — Citadels Shattered (0 AC)
With their masters imprisoned, the elemental citadels fail catastrophically; strongholds collapse and scars spread across the land. Stormglass appears where shattered citadels and warped ground fuse into lightning-scarred crystalline ruin-fields.

- 1.11.5 — The Crystal Vanishes (0 AC)
In the aftermath, the Calimemnon Crystal disappears, becoming a lingering danger and mystery in the new age.
